Meghan Wu imagined the place where they kept her friend’s body would be dark and cold. But the room was filled with yellow light. There was a slight floral scent in the air from the flowers on display.

Wu saw Yaolin Wang lying on the dressing table, covered by a cream colored blanket. Yaolin’s eyes were closed, eyelashes curled neatly. Wu was almost convinced Yaolin was just sleeping.

“She’s not dead,” Wu thought.

If Wu were alone, she would have called Yaolin’s name over and over until she woke up.

When friends first heard the news that Yaolin was dead, they assumed Daniel Xiao was her killer, even before police confirmed.

A close friend heard her crying when Xiao locked her out of her apartment. A roommate in Seattle saw the welts he left on her body. A friend in Bloomington let her stay in her apartment when she needed shelter.

When her friends put the bits information they each had together, the image of an abuser came into focus. Now, they helplessly relive the moments that could have kept Yaolin alive.
